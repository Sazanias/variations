Sensation

                                                                                                        by Arthur Rimbaud



    Through blue summer nights I will pass along paths,                                    Not a word, not a thought:
    Pricked by wheat, trampling short grass:                                                       Boundless love will surge through my soul,
    Dreaming, I will feel coolness underfoot,                                                        And I will wander far away, a vagabond
    Will let breezes bathe my bare head.                                                               In Nature - as happily as with a woman.
